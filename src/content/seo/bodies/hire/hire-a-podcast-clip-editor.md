## What is a podcast clip editor?

A podcast clip editor watches your full episodes (video or audio-with-video), finds high-retention moments, and edits them into vertical shorts. Strong podcast clippers understand:

- **Hook-first pacing** — the best quote or tension beat in the first 2–3 seconds
- **Talking-head framing** — jump cuts, zooms, and caption timing that keep eyes on screen
- **Episode context** — which guest lines stand alone without the full conversation
- **Brand consistency** — your caption style, lower-thirds, and thumbnail frames week over week

They are not general long-form editors. They optimise for repurposing audio-first content into short-form discovery.

## FAQ

### How many clips should I get per podcast episode?

Most interview shows aim for **8–15 clips per hour-long episode**. Solo commentary podcasts might ship **5–10**. Start with a smaller trial batch (5 clips) to align on hook style before scaling to a full episode or weekly pipeline.

### Do podcast clip editors need video, or is audio enough?

Video is strongly preferred — even a static wide shot with captions outperforms audio-only on TikTok and Reels. If you only have audio, say so in your job post; some editors add waveform visuals or stock B-roll, but that changes scope and price.

### Should I hire one editor or rotate freelancers?

Creators scaling past one episode per week usually **stick with one clipper** who learns guest names, recurring bits, and your caption rules. Kreatli Marketplace supports ongoing relationships — post a retainer-style job once you find the right fit.

### Is this the same as content-rewards clipping?

No. Content-rewards programs pay clippers based on views on **their** accounts. On Kreatli Marketplace, you hire an editor for **fixed project pay** and clips go to **your** channels.
