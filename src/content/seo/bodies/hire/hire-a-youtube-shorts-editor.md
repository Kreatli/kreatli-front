## What is a YouTube Shorts editor?

A Shorts editor repurposes your long-form YouTube content — tutorials, vlogs, podcasts-on-YouTube, livestream VODs — into vertical clips optimised for the Shorts shelf and suggested feed. They focus on:

- **Retention architecture** — pattern interrupts, text hooks, and cut density in the first 5 seconds
- **9:16 reframing** — centreing subjects, safe zones for UI overlays, no critical text cropped
- **Chapter and timestamp literacy** — pulling segments that make sense without the full video
- **Shorts-native captions** — large, high-contrast burned-in text (many viewers watch muted)
- **Batch consistency** — same intro card, end screen behaviour, and brand colours across a week of uploads

Unlike a general YouTube editor who might only touch 16:9 long-form, a Shorts specialist thinks in **daily or weekly clip quotas**.

## FAQ

### Can a Shorts editor also handle my long-form YouTube videos?

Some editors do both, but clipping and long-form are different skills. For Shorts scale, hire someone whose samples show **vertical retention editing**. If you need a single editor for everything, say so in your job post — expect a higher rate or longer turnaround.

### How do I brief an editor on which moments to clip?

Send **timestamps or chapter markers** when you have them, plus 2–3 example Shorts (yours or competitors') that match your target pacing. Many creators share a Notion doc or spreadsheet: episode link, must-clip moments, and "do not use" sections (sponsor reads, off-topic tangents).

### Should Shorts editors post on my channel?

On Kreatli Marketplace, editors **deliver files** — you retain publishing control. That keeps your channel auth, analytics, and monetisation under your account. If you want someone to upload and schedule, mention it explicitly in the brief; not every editor offers that.

### How is this different from AI clipping tools?

AI tools (OpusClip, etc.) generate fast drafts from transcripts. Human Shorts editors curate **brand voice, joke timing, and visual polish** AI often misses.
