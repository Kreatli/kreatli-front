# Hire a Twitch VOD Editor

A Twitch stream can contain dozens of clips worth posting, but the best moments are buried inside hours of VOD. **Kreatli Marketplace** helps streamers and creator teams hire Twitch VOD editors who can turn long broadcasts into Shorts, TikToks, Reels, highlight videos, and weekly content batches.

## What does a Twitch VOD editor do?

A Twitch VOD editor watches or scans stream recordings, finds moments with standalone value, and edits them into content for other platforms. The job blends editing skill with stream literacy. The editor needs to understand chat context, gameplay pacing, jokes, reactions, and the difference between a funny live moment and a clip that works for a cold viewer.

Common deliverables include:

- 15-60 second vertical clips for TikTok, Reels, and Shorts
- 3-10 minute YouTube highlight videos
- Stream recaps or "best moments" compilations
- Captioned reaction clips
- Gaming or Just Chatting moments
- Timestamp logs for future editing

## Why VOD editing is different

Regular video editors usually work from planned footage. Twitch VOD editors work from chaos: long recordings, chat interruptions, audio spikes, dead time, and moments that only make sense if the setup is included.

A good Twitch editor knows how to:

- Cut setup quickly without losing the joke
- Keep streamer personality intact
- Add captions or context cards where needed
- Avoid using private, off-brand, or sponsor-sensitive sections
- Build clips that make sense to non-live viewers
- Export for multiple platforms

If your channel depends on personality, do not hire only for flashy transitions. Hire for judgement.

## What to include in your job post

The more context you give, the less time an editor wastes guessing.

Include:

- Stream category: gaming, Just Chatting, reaction, podcast, IRL, music, or mixed
- Average VOD length
- How many clips or highlight videos you need per week
- Whether you provide timestamps
- Platforms you want to post on
- Examples of your best past clips
- Any topics, jokes, or moments to avoid
- Your preferred caption style and meme tolerance

If you already have a Discord, mod team, or chat clipping workflow, mention it. Some editors can work from community clip links instead of reviewing the full VOD.

## Pricing and workflow

Twitch VOD editing is usually priced by the batch because review time matters. A VOD with timestamps is cheaper than a four-hour stream where the editor must find every moment from scratch.

| Scope | Typical pricing logic |
| --- | --- |
| Timestamped vertical clips | Lower per-clip rate |
| Full VOD review | Higher batch rate |
| YouTube highlight video | Priced by finished length and complexity |
| Weekly stream package | Retainer or recurring milestone |

Simple vertical clips from timestamped moments may land around **$20-$45 per clip**. Full VOD review, gaming SFX, meme edits, and YouTube highlight packages can move higher quickly. Start with one stream or a small trial batch before committing to a weekly pipeline.

## How to evaluate VOD editor samples

Ask for examples that match your content type. A Valorant highlight editor and a Just Chatting editor may both be talented, but they make different choices.

Look for:

- Clean audio during loud moments
- Context preserved before the payoff
- Captions that do not cover gameplay UI
- Edits that match the streamer's humour
- Good moment selection, not just good effects
- Consistent exports for Shorts, TikTok, and Reels

If possible, give two editors the same short source segment as a paid test. The difference in judgement will be obvious.

## Build a repeatable VOD pipeline

The best Twitch editing setup is not a folder dump after every stream. Give the editor a repeatable system so each week gets faster.

Create a simple handoff:

- Stream date and category
- VOD link and local backup
- Community clip links, if any
- Must-use moments
- Moments to avoid
- Sponsor or moderation notes
- Target number of vertical clips
- Target number of YouTube highlights
- Deadline and priority platforms

If you stream often, ask the editor to return a timestamp log along with finished clips. Over time, that log becomes a content database: funny moments, strong reactions, recurring jokes, viewer questions, and segments that could become longer YouTube videos. This matters because Twitch content disappears quickly in the feed, but a good VOD workflow gives each stream a second life.

## Mistakes to avoid when hiring

Do not hire only from a flashy montage. Gaming and streaming edits need taste, but VOD work also needs patience. The editor must review source material, understand context, and make judgement calls about what should represent your channel.

Avoid these common mistakes:

- Sending four-hour VODs with no guidance and expecting low per-clip pricing
- Asking for every platform but not naming export specs
- Forgetting to mention alerts, music, or audio issues
- Allowing captions to cover gameplay, chat, or webcam reactions
- Treating YouTube highlights and Shorts as the same edit
- Skipping a paid trial before committing to weekly work

Your first job post should feel like the start of a production system, not a one-off rescue mission.

## FAQ

### Do I need to provide timestamps?

No, but timestamps reduce cost and turnaround. If you cannot timestamp, ask the editor to include VOD review in the quote.

### Can one editor make both Shorts and YouTube highlights?

Often, yes. Clarify whether you want vertical clips, horizontal highlights, or both. They require different pacing and exports.

### How fast should VOD clips be delivered?

For streamers, speed matters. Many creators aim to post clips within 24-72 hours while moments still feel current. Rush timelines cost more.

### How do I start?

Post a job with one recent VOD, your best past clips, target platforms, and the number of deliverables you want. A clear first batch gives editors enough context to quote accurately.
