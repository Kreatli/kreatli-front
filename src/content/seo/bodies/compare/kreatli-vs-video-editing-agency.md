# Kreatli Marketplace vs. a Video Editing Agency

A **video editing agency** is a managed service: you pay a retainer or package fee, and an account team coordinates editors, revisions, and delivery. **Kreatli Marketplace** lets you hire clip editors directly — you choose the person, agree the scope, and pay project rates without agency markup.

Agencies trade cost and speed for hand-holding. Direct hiring trades DIY coordination for lower prices and faster iteration.

## Quick comparison

| Factor | Kreatli Marketplace | Video editing agency |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Cost structure | Direct freelance rates ($15–$75+/clip typical) | Retainer + agency margin (often 30–100% markup) |
| Payment model | Per project / milestone with your editor | Monthly retainer or packaged tiers |
| Vetting | You review clipper profiles directly | Agency vets internally — opaque process |
| Turnaround | Agree with your editor (often 24–72 hrs) | Account-manager queue — can add days |
| Communication | Direct with your editor | Through account manager layer |
| Best for | Creators who want control and value | Teams that want zero hiring overhead |

## What is a video editing agency?

Agencies sell **managed editing**: you send footage, they assign an editor (or team), handle revisions, and deliver assets on a schedule. Pricing is usually retainer-based — $2,000–$10,000+/month depending on volume, niche, and service level.

You are paying for **coordination, QA, and backup capacity**, not just the edit itself. That overhead makes sense for brands with legal review, multi-stakeholder approval, or no time to brief freelancers.

## Where agencies work well

Agencies fit when:

- You need **white-glove service** and someone else to manage editor churn
- Multiple stakeholders approve every clip (legal, brand, marketing)
- Budget allows **premium pricing** for reduced founder time
- You want a **single invoice** instead of multiple freelancers

Enterprise brands and funded startups often prefer this model.

## Where agencies fall short for clipping

Short-form clipping for creators has different economics than agency retainers:

- **Markup on high volume** — 30 clips/week at agency rates adds up fast
- **Slower feedback loops** — revision requests pass through account managers
- **Generic editors** — agency rosters may not specialize in your niche (podcast, gaming, finance)
- **Overkill for solo creators** — you may not need a team when one great clipper suffices
- **Lock-in** — canceling a retainer is harder than pausing freelance jobs

Creators scaling YouTube Shorts or TikTok often outgrow agency minimums before they outgrow the need for clips.

## How Kreatli Marketplace is different

Kreatli Marketplace removes the agency middle layer while keeping structure:

1. **Post a clipping job** with deliverables, style references, and turnaround.
2. **Review clipper profiles** — see portfolios, niches, and past work yourself.
3. **Hire directly** and communicate without an account-manager buffer.
4. **Pay project rates** — no retainer minimum, scale up or down weekly.

You keep **agency-quality specialization** (niche clippers) without **agency-style markup and queues**.

## Who should use which?

**Choose a video editing agency** if you have budget for managed service, need multi-stakeholder workflows, and want zero involvement in hiring or briefing editors.

**Choose Kreatli Marketplace** if you are a creator or small team scaling Shorts/Reels, want direct relationships with clip editors, and prefer paying for clips rather than coordination overhead.

**Middle path:** hire one dedicated clipper on Kreatli Marketplace and treat them like your "agency of one" — same weekly cadence, fraction of the cost.

## Verdict

Agencies sell **managed editing as a service**. Kreatli Marketplace sells **direct access to clipping specialists**.
