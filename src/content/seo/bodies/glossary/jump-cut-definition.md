# What Is a Jump Cut?

A **jump cut** is an edit where the same shot continues but time is removed — the subject appears to "jump" forward in place. In short-form video, jump cuts are the default tool for tightening talking-head clips: cut dead air, mistakes, and pauses without changing camera angle.

Clip editors on **Kreatli Marketplace** use jump cuts constantly when repurposing podcasts and tutorials into TikToks, Reels, and Shorts under 60 seconds.

## Why it matters for creators and clippers

Long-form content is full of filler — "um," breaths, false starts, tangents. Viewers on TikTok will not sit through it. Jump cuts:

- **Compress runtime** — a 4-minute rant becomes a 40-second clip
- **Increase pace** — energy stays high without speeding up audio unnaturally
- **Highlight the best lines** — stitch multiple strong moments from one take
- **Signal "short-form native"** — the choppy rhythm viewers expect from creators like Gary Vee or podcast clip channels

Done poorly, jump cuts feel jarring or cheap. Done well, they are invisible — or intentionally stylistic.

## How jump cuts work

The editor keeps one camera angle and deletes sections of the timeline:

```
[Speaker starts sentence] — CUT — [Speaker mid-sentence] — CUT — [Speaker lands punchline]
```

The background does not change, so the speaker's position shifts slightly — the "jump." Editors often add:

- **Subtle zoom punches** — alternate 100% and 105% scale to disguise jumps
- **B-roll overlays** — cover the visual jump with supplementary footage
- **Sound design** — whoosh or click transitions between cuts

## Jump cuts vs. other edits

| Technique | What changes | Common use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Jump cut | Time removed, same angle | Talking-head tightening |
| Match cut | Visual element links two shots | Creative transitions |
| J-cut / L-cut | Audio leads or trails video | Podcast polish |
| B-roll cutaway | Different footage covers the edit | Hiding aggressive jumps |

Jump cuts are the fastest way to edit; cutaways take more time but look smoother.

## Examples

**Podcast clip:** A 90-second answer includes three strong points buried in rambling. The editor jump-cuts between each point, removing 45 seconds of filler. Final clip: 38 seconds, three hooks.

**Tutorial clip:** A creator stumbles over a product name twice. Jump cuts skip to the third clean take — the viewer never sees the mistakes.

**Stylistic choice:** Some editors leave visible jumps intentional — a rhythmic "head bob" effect popular in commentary and reaction Shorts.

## FAQ

### Are jump cuts bad editing?

Not in short-form. They are a **genre convention** for vertical talking-head content. Documentary and cinematic work avoids them; TikTok and YouTube Shorts embrace them. Match the style to the platform.

### How many jump cuts belong in a 60-second clip?

There is no fixed rule. Fast-paced commentary might have **15–25 cuts**; a thoughtful monologue might use **5–8** with longer holds. If the clip feels seasick, add B-roll or zoom variation — or give moments more breathing room.

### Should creators film differently for jump-cut editing?

Yes. **Pause briefly** between key points so editors have clean cut points. Avoid rocking in your chair — movement makes jumps more obvious. Multiple takes of the same line give editors options without ugly splices.
