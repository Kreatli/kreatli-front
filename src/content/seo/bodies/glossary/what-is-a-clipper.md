# What Is a Clipper? (Video Editing Definition)

A **clipper** is someone who turns long-form video — podcasts, streams, webinars, or YouTube uploads — into short-form clips for TikTok, Reels, Shorts, and similar platforms. The word means two different jobs in today's creator economy, so context matters before you hire or become one.

On **Kreatli Marketplace**, a clipper is a freelance video editor who produces finished short clips for a creator's channel at agreed project pay — not someone paid per view to post on their own account.

## Why it matters for creators and clippers

Search results mix both definitions. Creators looking to **hire editing help** often land on CPM/content-rewards platforms by mistake. Editors looking for **stable freelance income** may sign up for view-based programs and discover pay swings wildly week to week.

Knowing which "clipper" you mean saves time, money, and mismatched expectations. If you want clips on your channel, you need a freelance clip editor. If you want distributed promotion, you are in content-rewards territory — a different model entirely.

## The two types of clipper

### 1. Freelance clip editor (labour hire)

This is the Kreatli Marketplace model:

- A creator posts a job with deliverables, style, and turnaround
- The clipper edits source footage into vertical shorts
- Finished files go to the **creator's channel**
- Pay is **fixed per project or milestone** — typically $15–$75+ per clip depending on complexity

Think video editor specialising in short-form repurposing.

### 2. CPM / content-rewards clipper (distribution play)

Platforms like Whop Clips, Vyro, and similar programs use "clipper" differently:

- The clipper edits **and posts** on their own social account
- Earnings depend on **views, CPM, or campaign rules**
- Income is **variable** — strong edits can still earn little if reach falls short
- The creator funds a reward pool, not a per-deliverable hire

Think affiliate-style clipping at volume, not a production pipeline.

## Examples

**Freelance clipper scenario:** A podcast host uploads three hour-long episodes per week. Their clipper delivers 12 vertical clips with burned-in captions and hook-first pacing to the host's TikTok and YouTube Shorts — $40 per clip, due every Friday.

**CPM clipper scenario:** A brand launches a content-rewards campaign. Fifty clippers repurpose campaign footage, post from their own accounts, and earn based on view thresholds. One clip goes viral; most earn under $5.

Same word, opposite economics.

## FAQ

### Is a clipper the same as a video editor?

Not always. A general video editor might handle long-form YouTube episodes, ads, or documentaries. A **clipper** specifically focuses on short-form output — vertical aspect ratio, hooks, captions, and retention pacing — usually sourced from longer content. On Kreatli Marketplace, clippers are editors hired for that repurposing workflow.

### How much do freelance clippers charge?

Rates vary by niche and complexity. Simple talking-head podcast clips often run **$15–$35 per clip**. Gaming highlights with sound design and motion graphics can reach **$50–$75+**. Retainer arrangements for 20–30 clips per week are common among growing channels.

### Do clippers post the clips themselves?

On Kreatli Marketplace, **no** — the clipper delivers files to the creator, who publishes on their own channels. On CPM/content-rewards platforms, clippers typically post on their own accounts to earn view-based pay. Always confirm which model a platform uses before signing up or hiring.
