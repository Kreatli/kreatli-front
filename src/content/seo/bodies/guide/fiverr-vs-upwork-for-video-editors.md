# Fiverr vs. Upwork vs. Kreatli Marketplace: Where Should Editors Actually Start?

**Fiverr** is fastest for packaged one-off gigs. **Upwork** suits hourly and longer contracts with Connects overhead. **Kreatli Marketplace** focuses on **clipping jobs** — short-form, vertical deliverables, fixed project pay between creators and clip editors.

If your goal is sustainable clip editing income (not generic motion graphics sporadically), the best platform is the one where **buyers already understand hooks, 9:16, and per-clip scope** — not where you fight 500 unrelated "video editing" listings.

## Quick comparison

| Factor | Fiverr | Upwork | Kreatli Marketplace |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Focus** | General gigs | Broad freelance | Clipping-first |
| **Buyer intent** | Pre-packaged orders | Mixed, often hourly | Post clipping jobs |
| **Seller fees** | ~20% service fee | Sliding fee + Connects cost | Built for clipping workflows |
| **Competition** | Very high, global low bids | High, proposal grind | Niche, clip-specific |
| **Typical clip work** | One-off gig tiers | Sometimes mis-scoped | Per-clip / retainer batches |

| **Best for editors** | Quick first sale, spec gigs | Longer non-clip contracts | Repeat clip clients |

For creator-side comparisons, see [Kreatli vs Fiverr](/compare/kreatli-vs-fiverr) and [Kreatli vs Upwork](/compare/kreatli-vs-upwork).

## Fiverr: pros and cons for clip editors

**Works when:**

- You need a **first testimonial** fast
- You sell a crystal-clear package ("5 podcast clips, 48h delivery")
- You tolerate algorithm and fee pressure early career

**Breaks when:**

- Clients expect unlimited revisions for $15
- Buyers want 30 Shorts/week but buy a single Basic gig
- 20% fees push you to rush — quality drops, ratings suffer

Fiverr trains buyers to think in **commodity gigs**, not weekly pipelines.

## Upwork: pros and cons for clip editors

**Works when:**

- Client posts a **well-scoped** fixed-price clipping contract
- You already have Connects budget and strong proposal history
- Project is adjacent (long-form + clips) with clear milestones

**Breaks when:**

- You burn Connects on vague "need video editor" posts
- Clients want hourly tracking for work that should be per-deliverable
- Competition from global generalists undercuts niche pricing

Upwork rewards proposal volume; clip editors often prefer **deliverable clarity** over hourly drift.

## Kreatli Marketplace: pros and cons for clip editors

**Works when:**

- You specialise in **podcast, Shorts, TikTok, gaming, or stream** clipping
- You want **project pay** without view-based gambling
- You are building **repeat retainers** with creators

**Breaks when:**

- You only do wedding films or corporate long-form with no short-form samples
- You expect passive inbound without portfolio effort
- You want CPM/content-rewards posting on your own account — different model entirely ([clipping marketplace vs editor marketplace](/compare/clipping-marketplace-vs-video-editor-marketplace))

Kreatli is not "Fiverr with another skin" — buyers arrive with **clipping intent**.

## A practical starter strategy (2026)

1. **Build 3–5 vertical samples** in one niche ([portfolio guide](/guides/become-a-freelance-video-editor))
2. **Set rates** using [2026 benchmarks](/guides/how-to-price-video-editing-services)
3. **Primary platform:** Kreatli for clipping jobs + retainers
4. **Optional:** one tight Fiverr gig for social proof if you need a first star rating
5. **Upwork:** selective fixed-price posts only — do not spam Connects

Re-evaluate after **10 completed clipping projects**. Double down where repeat clients appear.

## How to win on Kreatli as a new editor

1. **Join as a clipper** with niche samples — not empty profiles
2. **Apply with specifics** — "10 clips/episode, $26/clip, Friday delivery"
3. **Start with trial batches** — creators test before retainers; nail the trial
4. **Collect repeat work** — reliability beats platform hopping

## Who should use which?

**Choose Fiverr** if you need one fast, small sale and a packaged offer — accept fee and commoditisation tradeoffs.

**Choose Upwork** if you pursue broader freelance contracts beyond clipping and can afford Connects + proposal time.

**Choose Kreatli Marketplace** if you are a clip editor (or want to be one) earning **fixed project pay** from creators who publish on their own channels — and you want buyers who already speak Shorts, hooks, and captions.

## Verdict

All three can produce income. For **clip editing as a career**, Kreatli Marketplace aligns buyer scope, deliverable format, and payment model with the work you actually do. Fiverr and Upwork remain useful side doors — not always the best home for 20-clips-per-week retainers.

## FAQ

### Can I use all three at once?

Yes, but **one niche brand** across platforms. Inconsistent samples confuse buyers. Prioritise where repeat clients cluster after 90 days.

### Which has the lowest fees?

Fee structures change — calculate **net per clip** after platform cuts, Connects, and revision time. A higher sticker rate with lower friction often nets more.

### Is Kreatli for CPM clippers?

No. Kreatli Marketplace is **editor hire** with fixed project pay. CPM/content-rewards clipping is a different job — see [CPM meaning in clipping](/glossary/cpm-meaning-clipping).

### I have no clients yet — where first?

Build samples, then apply to **niche clipping jobs** on Kreatli. Optional Fiverr gig for first review. Read [find your first client](/guides/find-first-video-editing-client).
